# RB-005.6 Account & Endpoint Containment

## Objective

Contain systems, identities, and services involved in exfiltration activity to prevent continued data exposure or attacker access.

---

## Status

Live

---

## Inputs

- Authentication telemetry
- Endpoint telemetry
- SaaS audit logs
- Transfer telemetry
- Session activity

---

## Tools

- Identity provider
- EDR platform
- SIEM
- Firewall/proxy platform
- SaaS administration consoles

---

## Steps

### 1. Identify Active Risk

Determine:
- Is exfiltration ongoing?
- Are active attacker sessions present?
- Are privileged accounts involved?
- Are multiple systems impacted?

---

### 2. Contain Impacted Accounts

Perform:
- Account disablement
- Session revocation
- Token invalidation
- Password reset
- MFA reset where required

---

### 3. Isolate Impacted Endpoints

Perform:
- EDR isolation
- Network quarantine
- VPN restriction
- Outbound traffic restriction

---

### 4. Restrict Exfiltration Channels

Block:
- Cloud storage destinations
- Suspicious domains/IPs
- File-sharing services
- Unapproved transfer protocols

---

### 5. Preserve Evidence

Preserve:
- Transfer logs
- Session telemetry
- Endpoint telemetry
- Cloud audit logs
- Staged files

---

### 6. Validate Containment Effectiveness

Confirm:
- No ongoing transfers
- Sessions terminated
- Outbound access restricted
- Additional spread prevented

---

## Decision Points

| Condition | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Active exfiltration persists | Escalate containment |
| Privileged account involved | Escalate to PB-009 |
| Additional systems identified | Expand containment scope |

---

## Output

- Containment status
- Impacted account inventory
- Endpoint isolation status
- Transfer restriction status

---

## Common Failure Modes

- Resetting passwords without session revocation
- Failing to isolate endpoints
- Ignoring SaaS-based persistence

---

## Automation Hooks

- Auto-account disablement
- Auto-session revocation
- Auto-endpoint isolation
- Auto-domain blocking
