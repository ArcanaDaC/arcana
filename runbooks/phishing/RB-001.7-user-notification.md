# RB-001.7 User Notification

## Objective
Provide timely and accurate communication to affected users and stakeholders regarding phishing activity and required actions.

---

## Inputs

- Incident summary
- Impacted user list
- Containment actions completed
- Required user actions
- Approved communication templates

---

## Tools

- Email platform
- Internal messaging platform
- Incident management platform
- Communication templates

---

## Steps

### 1. Identify Communication Scope

Determine:

- Single user
- Multiple users
- Department-wide
- Organisation-wide

---

### 2. Prepare Notification Content

Include:

- What happened
- What actions were taken
- What users need to do
- What to avoid
- Reporting guidance

Avoid:

- Excessive technical detail
- Unverified assumptions
- Sensitive investigation details

---

### 3. Notify Affected Users

Send communication to:

- Directly impacted users
- Managers (if required)
- Leadership (if required)

---

### 4. Provide Required User Actions

Examples:

- Password reset
- MFA re-registration
- Device reboot
- Reporting suspicious follow-on emails

---

### 5. Publish Broader Advisory (If Required)

If campaign is widespread:

- Share indicators
- Provide screenshots/examples
- Reinforce reporting process

---

### 6. Monitor User Responses

Track:

- Additional reports
- Questions
- Newly identified impacted users

---

## Decision Points

| Condition | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Large-scale campaign | Org-wide advisory |
| Executives targeted | Leadership notification |
| Additional reports received | Expand incident scope |

---

## Output

- User notifications sent
- Advisory distributed (if applicable)
- Additional reports collected

---

## Automation Hooks

- Auto-send user notifications
- Auto-create awareness advisory
- Auto-track user acknowledgements

---

## Notes

- Clear communication reduces panic and repeat exposure
- Avoid blame-focused language
- Reinforce reporting culture
