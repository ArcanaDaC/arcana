## 1. Prerequisites

- Completion of Sensitive Data Impact Assessment
- Incident scope reasonably established
- Impacted datasets identified
- Data classification completed
- Legal and Compliance stakeholders identified
- Incident timeline available
- Incident documentation up to date
- Regulatory obligations documented (where applicable)

## 2. Step-by-Step Instructions

1. **Initiate Legal and Compliance Engagement**
   - Notify:
     - Legal Team
     - Privacy Team
     - Compliance Team
     - Risk Team (if applicable)
   - Provide current incident summary and impact assessment.

2. **Provide Incident Documentation**
   - Share:
     - Incident timeline
     - Investigation findings
     - Impact assessment
     - Containment status
     - Recovery status
   - Ensure stakeholders have access to current documentation.

3. **Identify Regulatory Requirements**
   - Determine applicable obligations based on:
     - Jurisdiction
     - Industry
     - Contractual requirements
     - Data classification
   - Document all applicable notification requirements.

4. **Assess Notification Thresholds**
   - Determine whether notification thresholds have been met.
   - Assess:
     - Volume of exposed data
     - Type of exposed data
     - Number of affected individuals
     - Likelihood of harm
   - Document assessment outcomes.

5. **Identify Impacted External Parties**
   - Determine whether the incident impacts:
     - Customers
     - Partners
     - Vendors
     - Government agencies
     - Regulators
   - Create a list of potentially affected parties.

6. **Coordinate Notification Planning**
   - Work with Legal and Compliance to determine:
     - Notification requirements
     - Notification timelines
     - Notification ownership
     - Notification approval processes
   - Document all decisions.

7. **Review Communication Content**
   - Review all proposed:
     - Customer communications
     - Partner communications
     - Regulatory notifications
     - Public statements
   - Ensure Legal approval is obtained before release.

8. **Track Regulatory Actions**
   - Document:
     - Notifications submitted
     - Submission dates
     - Case numbers
     - Regulator responses
     - Follow-up requests
   - Maintain a complete audit trail.

9. **Document Legal Decisions**
   - Record:
     - Notification decisions
     - Legal guidance received
     - Compliance assessments
     - Risk determinations
   - Attach supporting documentation to the incident record.

10. **Escalate and Hand Off**
    - Provide status updates to:
      - Incident Commander
      - Executive stakeholders
      - Legal and Compliance leadership
    - Ensure all regulatory and legal actions are tracked to completion.
    - Update the incident record with all actions performed.

## 3. Post-Action

- Ensure all regulatory obligations have been documented.
- Ensure all notifications are recorded and retained.
- Preserve all legal and compliance communications in accordance with organisational policy.
- Maintain a complete audit trail of decisions and actions.
- Attach all supporting documentation to the incident record.
- Participate in post-incident review activities as required.
