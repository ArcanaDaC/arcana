# RB-002.9 Threat Actor TTP Mapping

## Objective

Map observed ransomware behaviour and attacker activity to known threat actor tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs).

---

## Inputs

- IOC list
- Payload analysis
- Authentication telemetry
- EDR telemetry
- Threat intelligence reporting

---

## Tools

- Threat intelligence platforms
- MITRE ATT&CK
- SIEM
- EDR platform
- Malware analysis reports

---

## Steps

### 1. Review Observed Techniques

Identify:
- Initial access methods
- Credential access techniques
- Lateral movement methods
- Persistence mechanisms
- Exfiltration behaviour

---

### 2. Map TTPs to MITRE ATT&CK

Document:
- Technique IDs
- Sub-techniques
- Observed behaviours
- Detection opportunities

---

### 3. Correlate with Known Ransomware Groups

Compare against:
- Public reporting
- Threat intel feeds
- Known infrastructure overlaps
- Ransom note patterns

---

### 4. Identify Detection Gaps

Determine:
- Missed detections
- Delayed alerts
- Visibility gaps
- Logging deficiencies

---

### 5. Update Defensive Content

Create/update:
- Detection rules
- Hunt queries
- Block lists
- Threat intel references

---

## Decision Points

| Condition | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Known threat actor identified | Prioritise actor-specific detections |
| Novel behaviour observed | Escalate for deeper analysis |
| Detection gaps identified | Prioritise hardening activities |

---

## Output

- TTP mapping report
- MITRE ATT&CK coverage
- Threat actor assessment
- Detection improvement recommendations

---

## Common Failure Modes

- Focusing only on malware hashes
- Ignoring attacker operational behaviour
- Missing pre-encryption activity

---

## Automation Hooks

- Auto-map ATT&CK techniques
- Auto-correlate threat intel
- Auto-generate IOC feeds
